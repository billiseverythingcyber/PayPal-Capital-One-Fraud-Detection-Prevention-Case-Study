FRAUD RESPONSE WORKFLOW

Purpose:
This workflow outlines the operational fraud response procedures simulated within the PayPal-Style Fraud Detection & Risk Intelligence project.

Scenario:
The system detects elevated fraud activity during a high-volume holiday transaction window occurring on December 24, 2025.

Workflow Overview:

STEP 1 — Transaction Monitoring
Incoming transactions are monitored through fraud detection pipelines and dashboard systems.

Key monitoring indicators include:

- Elevated fraud labels
- High-risk geographic activity
- Unusual transaction spikes
- Repeated device activity
- Nighttime transaction anomalies
- Large transaction amounts

STEP 2 — Alert Generation
When predefined fraud thresholds are exceeded, the system simulates operational alerts including:

- Fraud spike alerts
- Geo-risk alerts
- Device anomaly alerts
- KPI deviation warnings
- Manual review escalation alerts

STEP 3 — Fraud Operations Review
Fraud analysts review flagged transactions using:

- Transaction history
- Device behavior
- Customer activity patterns
- Merchant concentration analysis
- Geographic risk analysis

Potential analyst actions include:

- Approve transaction
- Escalate for investigation
- Request additional verification
- Temporarily restrict account activity
- Flag transaction for manual review

STEP 4 — Executive Risk Visibility
Executive dashboards provide leadership teams with visibility into:

- Fraud exposure levels
- Fraud trend escalation
- Operational workload
- Financial loss risk
- Chargeback exposure
- Seasonal fraud behavior

STEP 5 — Model Evaluation & KPI Validation
Data science and analytics teams evaluate:

- Model performance
- Recall and precision metrics
- False positive rates
- KPI integrity
- Fraud classification accuracy
- Seasonal behavior drift

STEP 6 — Compliance & Audit Support
Compliance teams review fraud events for:

- Regulatory reporting readiness
- AML risk exposure
- Audit documentation
- Operational accountability
- Risk governance procedures

Business Objective:
The primary objective of the fraud response workflow is to reduce financial loss through early fraud detection, rapid escalation, and operational coordination.

Strategic Principle:
Early fraud detection and coordinated operational response reduce revenue leakage, chargeback exposure, and customer trust deterioration.
